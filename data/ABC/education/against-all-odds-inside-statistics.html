Against All Odds: Inside Statistics

This series maintains the same emphasis on doing statistics. Video modules take viewers on location to where people from all walks of life are using statistics in their work. Stages 4-6 Science and Geography

Series 1

Episode 1 Stemplots

Duration: 10 minutes 57 seconds10m

As a first step in visualizing data, we use stemplots to understand measurements taken by the U.S. Army when they size up soldiers in order to design well-fitting gear and supplies for modern warfighters.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 2 Histograms

Duration: 8 minutes 49 seconds8m

Meteorologists use histograms to map when lightning strikes and this visualisation technique helps them understand the data in new ways.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 3 Measures Of Center

Duration: 7 minutes 58 seconds7m

It's helpful to know the centre of a distribution - which is what the clerical workers in Colorado Springs found out in the 1980s when they campaigned for comparable wages for comparable work.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 4 Boxplots

Duration: 8 minutes 14 seconds8m

Using the example of hot dog calorie counts, we use boxplots to visualise the five-number summary and make comparisons between different types of frankfurters.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 5 Standard Deviation

Duration: 8 minutes 15 seconds8m

How can we compare sales at two franchises in the Wahoo's restaurant chain? Standard deviation helps us quantify the variability in sales.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 6 Normal Curves

Duration: 11 minutes 17 seconds11m

A nature preserve that's tracked bird migrations through New England for decades records tons of bird-related data; everything from wingspan measurements to arrival dates provides examples of normal distributions.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 7 Normal Calculations

Duration: 11 minutes 57 seconds11m

Visit the Boston Beanstalks club for tall people. Height is normally distributed and we can use membership cutoffs and population data to calculate z-scores.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 8 Checking Assumption Of Normality

Duration: 9 minutes 54 seconds9m

Production at Pete and Gerry's Organic Eggs provides a number of distributions that look normal - but are they?

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 9 Scatterplots

Duration: 8 minutes 7 seconds8m

Plotting annual numbers of Florida powerboat registrations and manatee killings suggests an uncomfortable relationship for the marine mammals.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 10 Fitting Lines To Data

Duration: 9 minutes 53 seconds9m

Winter snowpack in the Colorado Rockies can predict spring water supply. Plotting annual measurements in a scatterplot lets resource managers draw a regression line that helps them forecast water availability.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 11 Correlation

Duration: 9 minutes 58 seconds9m

Twin studies track how similar identical and fraternal twins are on various characteristics, even if they don't grow up together. Correlation lets researchers put a number on it.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 12 Two-Way Tables

Duration: 9 minutes 32 seconds9m

One city surveyed the happiness of its residents. Two-way tables help organise the data and tease out relationships between happiness levels and opinions about aspects of the city itself.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 13 The Question Of Causation

Duration: 13 minutes 14 seconds13m

This historical story describes how researchers untangled the relationship between smoking and lung cancer.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 14 Designing Experiments

Duration: 10 minutes 20 seconds10m

We move beyond observational studies - like one of marine life in the remote Line Islands - to designing experiments that manipulate various subject groups - as in the case of a medical study about osteoarthritis treatments.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 15 Census And Sampling

Duration: 8 minutes 53 seconds8m

The U.S. counts every resident every ten years - or at least tries to. Statisticians use sampling from a population as an alternative to a complete count, as utilised at a potato chip factory.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 16 Sample And Surveys

Duration: 9 minutes 14 seconds9m

A visit to the University of New Hampshire Survey Centre illustrates how pollsters create accurate surveys. They can then use details from their sample to make inferences about a whole population.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 17 Introduction To Probability

Duration: 10 minutes 28 seconds10m

Probability is the mathematics of chance behaviour - and can help predict events such as the daily weather, or whether an asteroid will collide with Earth.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 18 Probability Models

Duration: 9 minutes 41 seconds9m

Casinos are as well versed in probability as statisticians and probability models help them maintain the house advantage over gamblers.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 19 Random Variables

Duration: 10 minutes 16 seconds10m

The Challenger space shuttle disaster was blamed on faulty O-rings. How can probability calculations on random variables help predict the chances of this kind of failure?

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 20 Binomial Distributions

Duration: 10 minutes 36 seconds10m

Sickle cell disease is an example of binomial distribution in families with two parents who are carriers for this genetic trait.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 21 Sampling Distributions

Duration: 11 minutes 9 seconds11m

Heights of third graders in one class. Quality scores for circuit boards at a factory. Taking multiple samples allows us to visualise the sampling distribution of the sample mean.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 22 Control Charts

Duration: 8 minutes 43 seconds8m

This quality control method helped Quest Diagnostics streamline and improve their system for processing and testing lab samples so they could meet their nightly deadlines.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 23 Confidence Intervals

Duration: 9 minutes 10 seconds9m

A battery manufacturer tests just a sample of its product to verify its claims about battery life. A margin of error and a confidence level help quantify its accuracy.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 24 Tests Of Significance

Duration: 15 minutes 52 seconds15m

Is a newly-discovered poem really written by William Shakespeare? Using statistical analysis of his known word use, researchers set up null and alternative hypotheses to investigate.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 25 Small Sample Inference For One Mean

Duration: 11 minutes 38 seconds11m

A brewer uses this technique to monitor quality differences in multiple batches of the same beer.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 26 Comparing Two Means

Duration: 10 minutes 9 seconds10m

Comparing the activity and calorie expenditure levels of Western office workers and African hunter gatherers adds some surprising new data to the science of obesity.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 27 Inference For Proportions

Duration: 9 minutes 54 seconds9m

Managers have no clue what conditions actually motivate their workers best, as shown by research conducted by Teresa Amabile, host of the original Against All Odds.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 28 Inference For Two-Way Tables

Duration: 9 minutes 12 seconds9m

Host Dr. Pardis Sabeti's own research examines possible genetic resistance to deadly Lassa fever in West Africa. Using Inference for Two-Way Tables helps untangle potential relationships.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 29 Inference For Regression

Duration: 12 minutes 56 seconds12m

Historical story of how statisticians built the case against DDT as the culprit behind plummeting peregrine falcon population numbers.

This episode was published 4 years ago.

Episode 30 One-way ANOVA

Duration: 11 minutes 37 seconds11m

Does holding a heavier clipboard make you estimate that a jar of coins has more money in it than if you're holding a lighter clipboard? Psychologists use One-Way ANOVA to analyse the data from this experiment.

This episode was published 4 years ago.
