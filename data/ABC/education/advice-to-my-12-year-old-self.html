A range of talented Australians, including some of our most well-known Indigenous stars, offer advice to their 12-year-old selves. Stages 4-5 PDHPE.

Brooke Boney

Duration: 2 minutes 52 seconds2m 52s

Triple J newsreader Brooke Boney is a Gamilaroi Gomeroi woman who always wanted to tell stories.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Nakkiah Lui

Duration: 2 minutes 56 seconds2m 56s

Nakkiah Lui is a writer, actor and a Gamilaroi/Torres Strait Islander woman who discovered being an outsider made her who she is today.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Nayuka Gorrie

Duration: 2 minutes 48 seconds2m 48s

Nayuka Gorrie is a Kurnai/Gunai, Gunditjmara, Wiradjuri and Yorta Yorta woman who lives her dream writing for TV and online.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Stan Grant

Duration: 3 minutes 3 seconds3m 3s

Journalist Stan Grant is a Wiradjuri/Kamilaroi man who explains the importance of asking questions and celebrating the future.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Deborah Cheetham

Duration: 2 minutes 48 seconds2m 48s

Opera singer Deborah Cheetham is a Yorta Yorta woman who tells stories through her music.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Dallas Reedman

Duration: 2 minutes 32 seconds2m 32s

Dallas Reedman is a presenter and TV host on ABC ME. He has some great advice about fostering your creativity and how to stay focussed!

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Ilai Swindells

Duration: 2 minutes 8 seconds2m 8s

Ilai is an actor and presenter and believes in being true to yourself even when people might not agree with you.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Lawrence Gunatilaka

Duration: 2 minutes 28 seconds2m 28s

Lawrence is a TV host and presenter. His advice starts with being open minded and remembering to try out new experiences!

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Tom Ballard

Duration: 3 minutes 37 seconds3m 37s

Tom Ballard is a comedian and TV presenter who thinks that the coolest thing that you can be, is yourself.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Jimmy Barnes

Duration: 3 minutes 17 seconds3m 17s

Jimmy Barnes is a rock and roll singer, who talks about growing up being told to not show his true feelings.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Ben And Liam

Duration: 3 minutes 22 seconds3m 22s

Ben and Liam are the hilarious hosts of Triple Js Breakfast radio show. They share their top tips for boys today, and try to figure out what it means to 'be a man'.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Dr Karl

Duration: 2 minutes 57 seconds2m 57s

Dr Karl Kruszelnicki is a walking encyclopaedia of knowledge. He tells his 12 year old self to keep making mistakes, because if you're not making mistakes you're not making anything!

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Stan Grant

Duration: 3 minutes 7 seconds3m 7s

Journalist Stan Grant is a Wiradjuri/Kamilaroi man who learnt from his father the importance of being responsible for yourself.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Hunter Page-Lochard

Duration: 2 minutes 35 seconds2m 35s

Actor Hunter Page-Lochard has played many roles in film and tv, and gives his views on what bravery and being a man mean to him.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Ben Quilty

Duration: 3 minutes 23 seconds3m 23s

Ben Quilty is an award-winning Australian artist. His advice to his 12 year old self is to think about what he loves doing, and then ignore everyone else and keep doing it!

This episode was published 2 years ago.

David Walliams

Duration: 2 minutes 44 seconds2m 44s

International comedian and children's book author David Walliams reflects on his experiences as a 12 year old, and offers advice to 12 year olds today.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Tim Winton

Duration: 2 minutes 41 seconds2m 41s

Author Tim Winton tells us his top tips for boys today, and shares his views on what being brave really means.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Eddie Woo

Duration: 2 minutes 51 seconds2m 51s

Eddie Woo is a mathematics teacher and host of ABC Me's 'Teenage Boss', who thinks 12 year old Eddie would be pretty surprised to see where life has taken him so far!

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Aliir Aliir

Duration: 2 minutes 50 seconds2m 50s

Aliir Aliir is an AFL player from the Sydney Swans who remembers what it was like to still be learning English at 12-years-old, and how sport became his way of connecting with other kids.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Gus (Goose from Spawn Point)

Duration: 3 minutes 3m

Gus "Goose" Ronald is one of the hosts of Good Game Spawn Point, and knows his 12 year old self would be very excited to know he gets to play games for work!

This episode was published 2 years ago.
