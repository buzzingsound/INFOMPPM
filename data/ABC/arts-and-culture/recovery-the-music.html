Recovery: The Music

ABC TVABC ARTSARTS & CULTURE

On air from 1996 to 2000, Recovery clocked up 378 hours of television. With 370 odd bands appearing, this broad and varied selection of 20 songs is a mere drop in the ocean of the 880 songs that were performed live.

Related

  • Recovery: The Music & The Mayhem

Episodes

Jon Spencer Blues Explosion perform 2 Kindsa Love / Rocketship

Duration: 6 minutes 17 seconds6m

The infamous performance of 2 Kindsa Love / Rocketship by the Jon Spencer Blues Explosion, went to airon 6 September 1997 and stopped everyone in their tracks. Stick around for Dylan's hyperventilating reaction.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Regurgitator perform ! (The Song Formerly Known As)

Duration: 3 minutes 42 seconds3m 42s

A Recovery favourite, Regurgitator performed in studio 4 times throughout the years and played ! (The Song Formerly Known As) on 26 September 1998.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Magic Dirt perform She Riff

Duration: 4 minutes 51 seconds4m 51s

Magic Dirt close the show on 2 May 1998 with a blistering rendition of She-Riff on their third visit to the Recovery studios.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Frenzal Rhomb perform Mum's Changed The Locks / There's Your Dad

Duration: 3 minutes 56 seconds3m 56s

Frenzal Rhomb perform Mum's Changed the Locks and There's Your Dad on 29 November 1997, on the show guest hosted by Dave McCormack from Custard.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Vika and Linda Bull perform The Parting Song

Duration: 4 minutes 8 seconds4m 8s

Inductees to the Music Victoria Hall of Fame for 2019, Vika and Linda Bull performed The Parting Song on Recovery on 27 July 1996, providing some calm amongst the chaos on a day when there was a dog wash in studio.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

The Fauves perform Dogs Are The Best People

Duration: 2 minutes 52 seconds2m 52s

The Fauves help set the tone for a canine themed show, performing their song Dogs are the Best People on Recovery on 27 July 1996.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

The Clouds perform Here Now

Duration: 2 minutes 8 seconds2m 8s

The Clouds opened the Recovery episode of 22 February 1997 with their song Here, Now. Other guests on the show that day included Russell Crowe spruiking his band 30 Odd Foot of Grunts.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Gerling perform Death To The Apple Gerls

Duration: 5 minutes 51 seconds5m

Gerling perform Death to the Apple Gerls on the 31 October 1998 show that started with Dylan being abducted by aliens, leaving Jane Gazzo and Leigh Whannell to host the show.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

TISM perform Whatareya?

Duration: 3 minutes 52 seconds3m 52s

In an all-inclusive performance, This is Serious Mum aka TISM play Whatareya on the 4 July 1998 show. Keep an eye out for a security guard being dragged in and Speedy the Monkey making an appearance.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

The Superjesus perform Ashes

Duration: 3 minutes 54 seconds3m 54s

The Superjesus let loose on Ashes from their debut album Sumo, performed on their customised set on the 7 November 1998.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

The Living End perform Prisoner Of Society

Duration: 3 minutes 47 seconds3m 47s

The Living End perform their anthemic Prisoner of Society for the first time on Recovery on 11 October 1997.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

NoKTuRNL perform Unveiled

Duration: 3 minutes 41 seconds3m 41s

From Arrernte country, NoKTuRNL fused metal and hip hop to create their own sound. They appeared in studio at Recovery on 31 May 1997 playing Unveiled, and again the following year as the house band requested by Public Enemy.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

The Dirty Three perform Authentic Celestial Music

Duration: 5 minutes 11 seconds5m

The Dirty Three cast their magic over the Recovery studio with Authentic Celestial Music as introduced by guest hosts The Fauves on the 13 December 1997.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Frank Yamma performs Everybody's Talking

Duration: 3 minutes 34 seconds3m 34s

Frank Yamma performed his song Everybody's Talking in Pitjantjatjara language on Recovery, on 31 May 1997.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

The Brown Hornet perform Love

Duration: 4 minutes 37 seconds4m 37s

Dylan Lewis and his band The Brown Hornet play Love, on 14 November 1998, the last in-studio show and Dylan and Jane Gazzo's final Recovery episode. Roll Credits.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Nitocris perform Sycophant

Duration: 3 minutes 57 seconds3m 57s

The first song ever performed on Recovery, on the very first episode which broadcast on 20 April 1996, was Sychophant by Sydney band Nitocris with the memorable lyrics 'die yuppie yuppie yuppie die'.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

The Mavis's perform Cry

Duration: 4 minutes 46 seconds4m 46s

Recovery regulars The Mavis's perform their hit single Cry on the show on 30 May 1998.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

The Whitlams perform No Aphrodisiac

Duration: 4 minutes 44 seconds4m 44s

The Whitlams play their single No Aphrodisiac to the Recovery studio audience on Valentines Day 1998, not long after it was voted number 1 in the 1997 Triple J Hottest 100.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Tiddas perform Anthem

Duration: 2 minutes 46 seconds2m 46s

Tiddas performed their now classic song Anthem on Recovery on 3 August 1996. They were inducted into the National Indigenous Music Awards Hall of Fame in August 2019.

This episode was published 2 years ago.

Blind Boys of Alabama perform Do Lord

Duration: 3 minutes 21 seconds3m 21s

The Blind Boys of Alabama brought a little gospel to the Recovery studios on 2 November 1996, broadening the young ears of the studio audience with Do Lord.

This episode was published 2 years ago.
